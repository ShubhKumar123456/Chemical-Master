Prompt 1: What is the chemical formula for water?
Response 1: The chemical formula for water is H2O, consisting of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom.

Prompt 2: What is the molecular weight of carbon dioxide?
Response 2: The molecular weight of carbon dioxide (CO2) is approximately 44.01 g/mol.

Prompt 3: Can you explain what sulfuric acid is?
Response 3: Sulfuric acid (H2SO4) is a highly corrosive strong mineral acid, widely used in industry, particularly in the production of fertilizers and chemical manufacturing.

Prompt 4: What are the uses of sodium chloride?
Response 4: Sodium chloride (NaCl), commonly known as table salt, is used in food seasoning, de-icing roads, water softening, and various industrial processes.

Prompt 5: What is the pH value of hydrochloric acid?
Response 5: The pH value of hydrochloric acid (HCl) typically ranges from 0 to 1, depending on its concentration, making it a strong acid.

Prompt 6: What is ethanol used for?
Response 6: Ethanol (C2H5OH) is used as a solvent, in alcoholic beverages, in fuel (as bioethanol), and as a disinfectant in medical settings.

Prompt 7: What is the chemical formula of acetic acid?
Response 7: The chemical formula of acetic acid is CH3COOH. It is the main component of vinegar.

Prompt 8: Is ammonia a gas or a liquid?
Response 8: Ammonia (NH3) is a gas at room temperature but can be liquefied under pressure.

Prompt 9: How is calcium carbonate used in everyday life?
Response 9: Calcium carbonate (CaCO3) is used in the production of cement, as a calcium supplement, in toothpaste, and as a white pigment in paints.

Prompt 10: What is the main component of natural gas?
Response 10: The main component of natural gas is methane (CH4), which is a hydrocarbon used as a fuel.

Prompt 11: What is the difference between organic and inorganic compounds?
Response 11: Organic compounds contain carbon-hydrogen (C-H) bonds, typically found in living organisms, while inorganic compounds do not usually contain C-H bonds and are derived from minerals or non-living matter.

Prompt 12: What is the use of potassium nitrate?
Response 12: Potassium nitrate (KNO3) is used in fertilizers, as a food preservative, and in the production of gunpowder.

Prompt 13: What is the IUPAC name of ethanol?
Response 13: The IUPAC name of ethanol is ethanol itself, which follows standard organic chemistry nomenclature for a two-carbon alcohol.

Prompt 14: What is the boiling point of methanol?
Response 14: The boiling point of methanol (CH3OH) is approximately 64.7°C (148.5°F).

Prompt 15: How does hydrogen peroxide decompose?
Response 15: Hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) decomposes into water (H2O) and oxygen (O2), especially in the presence of light or catalysts like manganese dioxide.

Prompt 16: What are noble gases?
Response 16: Noble gases are a group of elements in group 18 of the periodic table, including helium (He), neon (Ne), argon (Ar), krypton (Kr), xenon (Xe), and radon (Rn). They are chemically inert and rarely form compounds.

Prompt 17: What is sodium bicarbonate used for?
Response 17: Sodium bicarbonate (NaHCO3), also known as baking soda, is used in baking, as an antacid, in cleaning products, and for neutralizing acids.

Prompt 18: What is the chemical structure of benzene?
Response 18: Benzene (C6H6) has a ring structure consisting of six carbon atoms bonded in a planar hexagonal arrangement, with alternating double bonds, making it an aromatic hydrocarbon.

Prompt 19: How is chlorine gas harmful?
Response 19: Chlorine gas (Cl2) is toxic and corrosive, and exposure can cause respiratory issues, skin irritation, and damage to the mucous membranes. It is commonly used as a disinfectant in water treatment.

Prompt 20: What is phosphoric acid used for?
Response 20: Phosphoric acid (H3PO4) is used in the food industry, especially in soft drinks, as well as in fertilizers, cleaning agents, and pharmaceuticals.
